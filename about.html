I'm Katie, a UX/UI Designer in Washington D.C. I was born in Wisconsin and raised in Vermont. I'm a researcher, a writer, a musician, an artist: but what sets me apart from the crowd?

I've taken an unconventional path. My breadth of education and work experience has made me a more mature and inclusive designer. I have a B.A. in English literature, and believe clear and concise writing is an often-overlooked aspect of the design process. I worked as a paralegal straight out of college, building a skillset of organization, communication, and teamwork while uncovering a passion for advocacy. Most recently, I spent five years designing at a private school for students with learning differences, becoming a fierce proponent of accessibility and inclusivity.

My design training is recent and relevant. For the past year and a half, I've been completing a design apprenticeship with Bloc, driving the UX/UI design process from discovery/strategy through clickable prototype. I'm comfortable with participating in productive critiques, presenting ideas with confidence, and managing projects to meet deadlines.

I'm committed to making positive change. Professionally and personally, I'm driven by the motto, “Leave it better than you found it.” I'm an empathetic listener and work to identify and alleviate pain points, finding creative solutions to challenging problems.

Design isn't my only interest. When not working, I can be found reading science fiction and fantasy books, bouldering at Earth Treks, watching episodes of old Nickelodeon cartoons, or hiking with my dog Ziggy Stardust.
