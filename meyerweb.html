Meyer Website Redesign

My Roles: Research, User Testing/Interviews, Information Architecture, Visual Design

Meyer refined its mission and brand in 2019 to more clearly address issues of systemic racism. In connection with their rebranding process, they wanted a website that reflected the organization’s new focus while still paying homage to their history and roots. (currently in development)

01
Discovery

Building a Foundation

At the kick-off of this project, I led our interaction team in establishing a solid foundation upon which to build our design.

First, I conducted an audit of Meyer’s current website to determine areas of improvement and recommendations. Following the audit, I developed a usability test script that addressed a series of tasks for potential grantees, current grantees, and funders -- our key user personas for this website.

Over the course of three weeks, I interviewed six individuals who had ties to Meyer and the greater D.C. philanthropic community. These interviews proved integral to the later steps of my design process.

02
Architecture

Leading the Design

After completing our discovery phase, I was appointed lead designer for the scope of this project. I set out to address a number of improvements, including prioritizing Meyer's new mission/goal areas, creating clear pathways on the site to address various users' needs, and developing dynamic imagery that would help elevate succinct copy.

I began by writing user stories to identify the needs of Meyer’s key audience types. I used these stories to develop user flows and the new sitemap.

From Flows to Wireframes

Using Adobe XD, I used these flows to map out a full set of wireframes for Meyer’s new website. I created an interactive wireframe prototype to help Meyer internally test the site.

03
Visual Design

After iterating on the wireframes and collaborating with the client to refine the information architecture, I took the branding identity established by our creative team and applied it to create a full suite of mockups along with a clickable homepage walkthrough.

04
Takeaways

1. Be Bold.

Meyer’s redefined mission gave me an opening to suggest dramatic changes that resonated with the client. Had I stuck too closely to their prior website design or been swayed too much by their history in the region, we would not have ended up with the same level of success.

2. Plan for the hand-off.

With custom imagery, parallax, and a few tricky interactions in mind, I established a system of assets and video guides for hand-off to our developers. This helped communicate my vision to the engineers and ensure a seamless design-to-development hand-off.

3. Think mobile - early.

Our client was desktop-focused, which delayed my thought process into the website’s mobile layout. After this project, I began designing mobile in tandem with desktop, ensuring the best possible responsive experiences for all users.
